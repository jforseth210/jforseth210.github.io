The Top Five State Websites

Out of the Five I Actually Looked At

  1. Nebraska

    Overall a clean visually appealing website that seems to have a good idea of what it's visitors are actually looking for. The hover menu makes efficient use of space although doesn't work reliably on my device. Also, the public calendar appears to be broken on my device.

  2. Delaware

    Probably the website with the best color palette and fonts. It's focus on Coronavirus may detract from the experience of users looking for other information about the state.

  3. Florida

    Relatively minimal design, basically just a search bar. A bit sparse, but it works I suppose. Great cover photo, section looks good, footer is a little ugly. Certain elements like the "How can we help you?" in the search bar could use a bit more color contrast.

  4. Illinois

    Overall, it's not a bad website. It's relatively clean on top, if a little cluttered when you scroll down. Good font and relatively consistent color scheme. It's major failure is that the "Illinois Virtual Assistant" is not working. The Alerts are kinda clunky and I think it'd look better if the "State of the State" banner wasn't above the navbar.

  5. Alaska

    This site has two navbars, which is confusing. The hover 'Resident, Business, Visitor' menu is unintuitive and kind of ugly. The page is filled with links and most of the text is too small. Unrelated things collide, like translations and flag status.
