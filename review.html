Hosts Review

For this class, Github pages or some other free static host will work just fine. I like tinkering around serverside, hence the hosts I decided to compare:

Linode

Cons

  • Static hosting possible, not well documented
  • Fewer data centers than DigitalOcean

Pros

  • Wide selection of one click apps
  • Tailor-made setups for common uses like machine learning or video streaming
  • Real human support

Pricing

Heroku

Cons

  • Addon and App selection limited to developer utilites like databases.
  • No static hosting, although static sites can be served relatively simply using PHP.

Pros

  • Free dynamic site hosting
  • Easy to deploy
  • Works with variety of backend languages

Pricing (It's complicated!)

  • Good free tier
  • FREE
  • after that, pay only for what you need
  • $7-$8,700/mo

Digital Ocean

Cons

  • No free tier for dynamic sites
  • More difficult to deploy than Heroku

Pros

  • Fantastic documentation
  • Extremely high uptime
  • Many datacenters in many countries
  • Wide variety of products
  • Wide selection of one click apps to get started quickly

JForseth.tech

Cons

  • Abysmal bandwidth
  • Not available to the public
  • Maintained part-time by a college freshman with limited physical access to server hardware

Pros

  • 2 gb of RAM, 128 gb of storage, and some random arm-based CPU
  • Full server operating system (Ubuntu) with shell access.
  • One time payment, no monthly subscription (except existing internet subscription, of course)
  • Excellent learning experience
  • Server has close geographical proximity to Helena
  • Actually pretty good uptime. 100% this month.

Pricing

  • Raspberry Pi: $35ish
  • 128gb Micro SD Card: $17ish
  • Internet: Graciously provided by family

I chose this host because I am I cheapskate who likes tinkering with servers.
