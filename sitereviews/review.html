Site Review

For my sites, I chose MyBib, DuckDuckGo, and the AET. These are all sites that I use (or at least used) fairly regularly, so I have a decent idea of what I do and don't like about each.

Let's talk about MyBib first. This is a citation generation tool I found after the ads on Citation Machine made it almost unusable. Unlike citation machine, this site very useful and not riddled with ads. The layout is two column, with a main section that shows your Works Cited, with a sidebar for selecting various projects. Navigation is highly intuitive with common options like adding citations, selecting a citation format, collaborating with others, and exporting your citations being made available via a horizontal menu at the top. All options related to individual citations (including editing, copying a full citation or parenthetical citation, etc) can be done by clicking on an individual citation from within the bibliography. Modals have a nice flying-in animation. The color scheme is consistent and visually appealing. The code is a little complex, but pretty readable.

I don't have as much to say about DuckDuckGo. It has a nice, fairly minimal design. It shows you a search box. You type in a query and it shows you results. It does one thing and does it well. It had a variety of color schemes, the dark mode looks great. It has some great little features built in (a QR code generator, a translator, etc), as well as wikipedia information on a sidebar. The code is pretty readable. It doesn't really go over the top with colors and animations or anything. It just works.

The AET does not "just work". Navigation is a horrible mess of menus and sub-menus. Some of the pages I wanted to look at for this essay have entirely disappeared or are buried behind a menu that, despite four years of using this software I am unable to find. Some buttons don't look like they have any styling whatsoever, some elements don't have any padding. Some buttons are just icons with no tooltips, labels, or indication as to what they actually do. Popups look like they're straight out of Windows Vista. It uses tables for formatting and incomprehensible acronyms for class names. The top header, while being a complete waste of screen real-estate, does have a nice blue background color. Icons look good, I suppose, mostly consistent throughout. It doesn't really have any animations or anything. I would rather write recordkeeping software myself (no, seriously) than use the AET.

MyBib and DuckDuckGo both do one thing and do it well. When I'm using them, I don't have to think about it. They get out of the way and let me worry about the problem at hand: My paper or my question or whatever it is I'm working on. The AET does a lot of things, poorly. When I use it, it is the problem at hand. The records themselves are secondary to the problem of trying to figure out how to get it into the AET. All of these sites are designed to serve a purpose. They aren't here to entertain or provide information, they solve a problem. When done well, they get out of your way, you don't have to think about them. When done poorly, they become problems themselves.
